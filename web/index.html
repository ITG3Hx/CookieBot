Auto-responder

When a message contains a trigger word, the bot replies automatically. Handy for FAQ answers like "ip" or "rules".

Replies support the same placeholders. Each trigger has a short cooldown so it can't be spammed.

Sticky messages

Keeps a message glued to the bottom of a channel by reposting it after people talk. Perfect for a rules reminder or your server IP.

One sticky per channel. It only reposts when someone else sends a message, so it never spams an idle channel.

Staff applications

Open the apply site ↗

Your applications live on a separate site at /apply. People apply there; your moderators review them at /apply/review. Configure it here.

Accepting applications

When off, the public apply page shows a "closed" notice and won't accept submissions.

Positions & roles

Turn positions on/off, edit their blurb, and map each one to the Discord role the bot grants when you accept someone. The bot's role must sit above the roles it assigns.

Department Leader tracks

Applicants for Department Leader pick which department to run. Map each one to its own Discord role, so a Helper-department lead and a Developer-department lead get different roles. A track with no role mapped falls back to the Department Leader position's own role.

On decision

Reviewer access

Two ways for moderators to log in at /apply/review, separate from your admin password, so mods only ever see applications.

Shared password

One password everyone on the review team uses.

One-time codes

Give each moderator their own single-use code instead. It stops working the instant they log in with it (or after 7 days if never used), so nothing is left to leak or share around.
